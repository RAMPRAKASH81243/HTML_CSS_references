Mythical Creatures

Dragron
A dragon is a mythical creature found in the folklore of cultures throughout the world. It is roughly serpentine in form, and often possesses features drawn from different animals, such as wings and claws.[1] In nearly all cultures, it is conceived of as a powerful and awe-inspiring creature.[2]
Phoenix
The word Phoenix most commonly refers to a legendary, immortal bird from Greek mythology that cyclically regenerates or is reborn from its own ashes. Symbolically, it represents resurrection, renewal, and hope
Werewolf
A werewolf, or lycanthrope, is a mythological or folkloric human with the ability to shapeshift into a wolf or a hybrid wolf-human creature. Driven by ancient curses or the bite of another wolf, they are most famously depicted transforming under the full moon and losing their human intellect
Vampire
A vampire is a mythological undead being that subsists by feeding on the vital essence, typically blood, of the living. Originating in Eastern European folklore, these creatures have evolved from bloated, rotting corpses into the pale, charismatic, and aristocratic monsters popularized by Bram Stoker's Dracula
